A step into 'As We May Think' at 65 and my goals for this course

This week I came across a reading which spoke about the published work As We May Think by Vannevar Bush. Simon Harper mentions that the condition with which the human brain operates well in is where there is an association between information [1]. This speaks directly to the idea proposed by Bush where people can link information across different articles and therefore lead other readers in following the thought process and linking of another person [2]. I found this to be an interesting topic because many of my experiences while researching topics on the internet consist of moving from one article to many other articles. I often look at related topics or words associated with what I am researching. This helps my thought and memory process as I can remember key words or articles that are related to the topic. I can draw up a picture in my thought process which becomes like a tree with linking branches. The tree represents the broad topic and the branches represent all the information and associated ideas to the broad topic.

The ever-growing amount of information that we as humans are producing can make it harder to remember and link to previous knowledge. I find that websites such as Wikipedia make use of this idea well where there are hyperlinks on words mentioned in articles published. A reader can hover over each word to read a brief description on the word or go to the page with more information relating to the word. I find this to be personally satisfactory as I can easily and quickly pause the reading on a topic to learn more about a related topic. This in turn can deepen my understanding on the original topic of interest.

Hyperlinks are just one way that an article or topic can link different bits of information together. The use of audio, video, or animation can help enhance links between associated information. This falls under hypermedia which Denise Tolhurst describes as the linking of multiple formats such as text, video, audio, and still or animated graphics [3]. The use of text but also including other forms of media such as images or audio to link information together. In hypermedia an interesting topic of discussion is the use of adaptive hypermedia which Peter Brusilovsky explains to be a way of collecting data from users and creating personalized links and information for the user [4]. Although this can be useful for the user at the given moment, I feel that if that user would want to be given new information entirely disregarding previous data collected the adaptive hypermedia models would need to account for this. This is mentioned as being user modelling however was not the focus of the paper. I would like to research and look more into user modelling in the adaptive media space.

The goals that I have for this course are to learn more about hypermedia and the uses it can have to individuals in engagement with information. The use of colour, text style and images and how this can influence different users to explore a website. I want to learn more about the development and history surrounding the progress of information sources on the web. The main goal that I have for this course would be to learn and create an exciting website that would contain my portfolio of work that I have done.
