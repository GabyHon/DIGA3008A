"You Say You Want a Revolution? Hypertext and the Laws of Media" and how it speaks to the internet today

Moulthrop mentions that hypertext aids in the enrichment and rate of consumption of information [1]. Information in the post-modern world is often catered to each individual user. The user can follow hypertext in their own interest and at their own pace. This enables users to have a specifically catered experience to themselves when accessing information while having the ability to consume information at a faster rate when compared to text that might have been published in a newspaper, book or other form of print.

The growing use of hypertext has led to the creation and collaboration of information regardless of hierarchical norms. This can be seen in blog posts or online forums where any individual that has access to the interest today has the ability to create and collaborate with other individuals on information. The collaboration brings about the sharing of knowledge without needing an individual to acquire expertise in a certain topic to gain access to that information or participate in sharing that information. However, with the ever-growing number of individuals collaborating on information, there does need to be a structure in place to discern information that may look credible and information that may be actually correct.

The internet today does however require an individual to determine what may be incorrect information, information that another individual may want to spread, and information that has been researched and confirmed. Hypertext has made it easier on social media platforms to share information, connect other individuals to topics, and connect different topics together. However, with the help of hypertext and hypermedia on social media platforms, information can easily be changed or misinterpreted. Individuals need to take precautions and develop skills to scrutinize information and the validity that a piece information may or may not hold.

Many of the websites that I want to take inspiration from are ones that make use of transitions. I want the transitions to be engaging to a user while not being too distracting.
