Justice and Ethics in artistic and programming practice

The way I would approach justice and ethics in my artistic and programming practice would be to firstly design with the intention to ensure that there is equal access to the creative and digital experiences of my artefacts. Costanza-Chock [1] argues that inclusivity must be built into design processes from the start and that the needs of historically marginalized groups should be the focus rather than retrofitting accessibility for a later time.

I think that another important aspect of justice and ethics in artistic and programming practice is for the developers and artists to remain accountable for any consequences that may come up from their produced work. This will ensure that any design choices made will avoid inadvertently contributing to systemic injustice as emphasized by Syal and Kramer [2]. This then talks to collaboration, whether there is online collaboration through open-source software or transparently sharing methodologies to identify any biases within artefacts.

Collaboration does not only have to occur in spaces such as online spaces but actually designing and creating with communities. This will help developers and artists or designers to design and create not only for a community but to understand what the community would benefit from. Dombrowski et al. [3] outline strategies for participatory design that emphasises reciprocity and enablement. This ensures that the affected groups would have direct influence over the technical and creative results. The resulting solution to issues or future issues while working alongside communities would reflect lived experiences from the community instead of assumptions that possibly could be made about the community.

Art and technology should look at the long-term impacts such as sustainability and ethical responsibility over commercial success that may only last for a short-term. Looking at the social impact, ethical innovation, and longevity of artists and developers' artefacts would ensure that the creative and digital work would contribute in a meaningful way to the future.
