Reference Material

The positioning of my content in the middle of each page is taken from three refernce websites: Hivetown, ArenaNet, and Honkai: Star Rail.

On the Hivetown website, I like the placing of the content in the middle of the page which allows me to read highlited text blocks while also being able to view transitions that may appear on the sides of the screen as well as keeping my eyes more relaxed. I don't need to read long lines of text across the whole page, instead I can focus on the short text blocks without straining my eyes from reading too far left or too far right. One design choice that I find a bit distracting is the navigation button that appears after scrolling down from the top of each page. At the top of each page there is a horizontal navigation bar to different pages on the website and when that is no longer visible I do like the appearance of the navigation list icon. However, when I click on the navigation icon, a transistion takes place which covers the entire screen. The same transistion takes place when the icon is clicked again. I find that the transistion become a bit distracting from the content of the page since it covers the whole page and blocks the view of the content.

The ArenaNet website makes use of the content placed in the middle of the page as well. This again helps me focus on the content of the page without straining to read across the entire page. I enjoy that the images use a transition as a splash of paint or colour to indicate that that item on the page has been viewed. There is also a sort of progress bar on the left side of the homepage which indicated to me that I am at a certain point in the list of items displayed. I also enjoy the use of this transistion across each page. It makes me feel as a user that I am making each page come to life when I visit the page. There is also the use of transition to the top of each page however it is not exaclty intuitive. Instead of an arrow pointing upwards, I can click on the page's title to scroll back to the top of the page. It is an interesting way that was used instead of the standard up pointing arrow.

On the Honaki: Star Rail website I like the center content, aligned images and navigation. This leads me to focus on each section of text or image that is being displayed. One thing that I do find a bit difficult to read is the bar on the right hand side of the homepage. It shows the icons for the social media platforms that I can click on to view the account pages for the game. However, the text has been placed vertically where I would need to tilt my head to read the text. The designers probably wanted to make sure the user knows that the links will lead to the offical accounts for the game but it does feel out of place to have the text in a different direction to the text everywhere else on the page.
